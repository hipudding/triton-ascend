# Community Technical Meeting

Triton-Ascend holds regular community technical meetings as the core decision-making mechanism for project governance. Major matters such as technical direction, large-scale proposal reviews, and version releases are discussed and decided in technical meetings, with votes cast by all `Maintainers`. Meetings are open to all community members, and anyone can participate in discussions, but decisions and voting are done only by `Maintainers`.

## Meeting Content

- Technical direction discussion and decision-making
- Large-scale proposal (RFC) review and voting
- Version release planning and timeline
- Community Issue and PR priority ranking
- Contributor nomination and removal discussions

## Voting Rules

- `Maintainers` who do not attend the meeting must provide their vote within 3 business days after the meeting; failure to vote within the deadline is considered abstention
- A vote is passed when more than half of the participating `Maintainers` agree (abstentions are not counted in the vote total)
- Voting results are recorded in the meeting minutes

## Decision-Making Process

### Routine Decisions

Routine decisions in daily development (such as `Bug` fixes, minor feature improvements) are reviewed and decided by the `Maintainer` of the relevant module in the `Issue` or `PR`, without requiring a meeting.

### Major Decisions

The following matters are considered major decisions and require collective discussion by `Maintainers` at community technical meetings:

- Major architectural changes
- Breaking `API` changes
- Adding core features or removing existing features
- Major upgrades to third-party dependencies
- Version release planning

Major decision process:

1. Submit an `RFC` (Request for Comments), describing the change content, impact scope, and implementation plan
2. Discuss at the technical meeting
3. `Maintainers` make a joint decision
